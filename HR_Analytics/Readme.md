## HR Analytics – Employee Attrition Analysis using SQL & Power BI 
![HR Analytics](https://github.com/NarayanDhanaji/SQL_Projects/blob/main/HR_Analytics/HR-analytics.jpg)

## Project Overview
-This project analyzes employee data to understand attrition patterns, 
salary trends, job satisfaction, and department-wise workforce insights
using SQL, Excel, and Power BI.

## Business Problem

- The organization faced high employee attrition but lacked visibility into:

- Which departments are most affected

- Key factors driving employee exits

- Demographic and role-based attrition trends

- This limited HR’s ability to take preventive actions.

## Business Goal

- To identify:

- Major drivers of employee attrition

- High-risk departments and job roles

- Salary, gender, and experience impact on attrition so HR teams can improve retention strategies.

## Dataset Information

- EmployeeNumber,	Age,	Gender,	MaritalStatus,	Attrition,	Department,	JobRole,	JobSatisfaction,
  JobLevel,	BusinessTravel,	DistanceFromHome,	Salary,	OverTime,	EnvironmentSatisfaction,
  TotalWorkingYears,	WorkLifeBalance,	YearsAtCompany,	YearsSinceLastPromotion

- Total records: ~1,470 employees

## Data type: Structured HR dataset

- Tools & Technologies

- SQL (Data extraction & analysis)

- Power BI (Dashboard & visualization)

- DAX (Calculated measures)

## Key KPIs

- Total Employees

- Total Attrition

- Attrition Rate (%)

- Average Salary

- Average Years at Company

## Key Insights

- Overall attrition rate is ~16%

- Research & Development has the highest attrition

- Employees with lower salary slabs show higher attrition

- Attrition is higher among employees with low job satisfaction

- Male employees show slightly higher attrition compared to female employees
